TouchTools is a gesture design language and recognition engine that invokes tools or commands by mimicking how we grab tools in the real world. It is a tiny step into the future of interaction design as envisioned by Bret Victor (we are, unfortunately, still touching glass).

A second contribution of TouchTools is the recognition algorithm. Rather than classifying on touch down, we wait 50ms after an initial touch to allow all touches to hit the screen, then compute rotation-invariant features on this set of points, and feed these into a support vector machine (SVM). Details are available in the paper.

To ensure credit is given where credit is due, much of the code for TouchTools was developed by Robert Xiao. This work was very much a collaboration and I'm honored to have been a part of it.

Demos of TouchTools are available for both iOS and Android. To see a demo of this work, please contact info@qeexo.com

Video Overview of TouchTools. Robert Xiao and I have additionally ported TouchTools to Android and Windows platforms, and have run the software on form factors ranging from tablets to interactive whiteboards.
